Benign Ovarian Disease
Question 1
What is the most common benign ovarian tumor in reproductive age women?
A) Serous cystadenoma.
B) Mature cystic teratoma (dermoid cyst).
C) Mucinous cystadenoma.
D) Fibroma.
E) Brenner tumor.
Correct Answer: B) Mature cystic teratoma (dermoid cyst).
Explanation: Dermoid cysts (mature cystic teratomas) are the most common benign ovarian tumors in women of reproductive age, accounting for approximately 20% of all ovarian neoplasms.

Question 2
Which tumor marker is most useful in monitoring epithelial ovarian cancer?
A) AFP.
B) CA-125.
C) CEA.
D) hCG.
E) LDH.
Correct Answer: B) CA-125.
Explanation: CA-125 is elevated in approximately 80% of epithelial ovarian cancers and is used for monitoring treatment response and recurrence.

What is the most appropriate initial investigation for a suspected ovarian mass?
A) CT scan.
B) MRI.
C) Transvaginal ultrasound.

E) PET scan.

Hypertensive Disorders in Pregnancy
Question 4
At what gestational age does preeclampsia typically present?
A) Before 10 weeks.
B) Between 10-20 weeks.
C) After 20 weeks.
D) Only during labor.
E) Only postpartum.
Correct Answer: C) After 20 weeks.
Explanation: Preeclampsia is defined as new-onset hypertension and proteinuria occurring after 20 weeks of gestation.

Question 5
What is the definitive treatment for eclampsia?
A) Magnesium sulfate.
B) Labetalol.
C) Delivery.
D) Nifedipine.
E) Hydralazine.
Correct Answer: C) Delivery.
Explanation: The definitive treatment for eclampsia is delivery of the fetus and placenta, as the condition resolves after removal of placental tissue.

Normal Labor and Delivery
Question 7
What is the definition of the active phase of the first stage of labor?
A) 0-3 cm dilation.
B) 4-10 cm dilation.
C) Full dilation to delivery.
D) Delivery to placental expulsion.
E) Onset of contractions.
Correct Answer: B) 4-10 cm dilation.
Explanation: The active phase begins at 4 cm cervical dilation and continues until full dilation (10 cm), characterized by regular contractions and progressive cervical change.

Question 8
What is the normal duration of the third stage of labor?
A) Up to 5 minutes.
B) Up to 15 minutes.
C) Up to 30 minutes.
D) Up to 60 minutes.
E) Up to 90 minutes.
Correct Answer: C) Up to 30 minutes.
Explanation: The third stage of labor (from delivery of the baby to delivery of the placenta) normally takes up to 30 minutes. Active management can reduce this to 5-15 minutes.
